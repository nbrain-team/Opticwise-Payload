Own Your Building’s Digital Infrastructure—Or Be Owned By It

Learn how owning your building’s digital infrastructure can give you a competitive edge and prevent being left behind.

April 2, 2025 · 3 min read

TL;DR: If you don't own your building's digital infrastructure, your vendors do. The choice isn't theoretical — it determines who controls the data, who captures the revenue, and who has leverage when the next vendor relationship breaks. CRE owners must take back digital infrastructure before AI cements the lock-in.

Let’s be real—your building isn’t just walls and windows anymore. It’s a fully connected, data-driven ecosystem. Yet, if you don’t own your digital infrastructure, you’re essentially renting your own technology backbone from a third party. That means lost revenue, weak building network security, and a serious lack of control over smart building technology.

What’s Digital Infrastructure, Anyway?

Think of it as your building’s central nervous system. Digital infrastructure includes physical digital infrastructure like fiber optics, network servers, and IoT sensors, plus all the software that makes building automation systems tick. If someone else owns it, they call the shots—not you.

The Game-Changing Benefits of Owning Your Digital Infrastructure

If you’re still letting a third party control your building’s digital backbone, it’s time to rethink your strategy. Here’s why ownership is a game-changer:

1. Control the Flow, Control the Show

A building digital infrastructure that you own means you decide how bandwidth is allocated. Want to prioritize tenant Wi-Fi, security systems, or building automation systems? You can. Relying on an ISP’s cookie-cutter setup? Good luck getting the performance you need when you need it.

💡 Stat Check: According to a WiredScore report, 91% of tenants consider reliable connectivity a top factor in leasing decisions (WiredScore, 2023). Without ownership, you’re at the mercy of third-party providers who may not meet these expectations.

2. Unlock New Revenue Streams

Did you know you could be making money off your building’s connectivity? Owners who control their digital infrastructure can offer premium tenant internet, private business networks, or other tech-driven services—turning their building intelligence into a profit engine. With OpticWise Building Intelligence, your network becomes an asset, not an expense.

💡 Stat Check: Smart buildings with robust building digital infrastructure can reduce operational costs by up to 30% while increasing property value (McKinsey & Company, 2022).

3. Fortify Your Cybersecurity (Because Hackers Love Easy Targets)

If you don’t own your building's network security, you’re relying on someone else to keep your data safe. That’s a gamble. By owning your digital backbone, you can implement top-tier cybersecurity, control access, and ensure tenant privacy. With OpticWise Building Intelligence, security isn’t optional—it’s built-in.

💡 Stat Check: Cybercrime is projected to cost businesses over $10.5 trillion annually by 2025 (Cybersecurity Ventures, 2023). Buildings with third-party-managed networks are at higher risk due to shared infrastructure vulnerabilities.

4. Build a Smarter, More Efficient Property

Smart building technology thrives on seamless integration, but that’s impossible if your digital infrastructure is locked down by a third party. When you own it, you can optimize energy usage, automate maintenance, and create a high-tech experience for tenants—all without limitations.

💡 Stat Check: Effective use of building automation systems can cut energy costs by up to 20% while enhancing occupant comfort and productivity (McKinsey & Company, 2023).

5. Future-Proof Your Investment

Technology moves fast. If you don’t own your building's digital infrastructure, you’re at the mercy of external providers when it’s time to upgrade. Ownership means you set the pace, ensuring your property stays ahead with the latest building intelligence solutions instead of falling behind the competition.

💡 Stat Check: Buildings with modernized digital infrastructure ownership see an increase in tenant retention by 15% and command higher rental rates (JLL, 2023).

Take Back Control of Your Building

Your building’s digital backbone should be an asset that works for you—not a liability that holds you back. Digital infrastructure ownership isn’t just about having better tech; it’s about driving revenue, enhancing security, and staying competitive in a world that’s only getting smarter.
