Data Infrastructure: The Engine Behind Modern CRE Operations

April 30, 2025 · 2 min read

TL;DR: Modern CRE operations run on data infrastructure, not BMS. The engine behind every operational decision — from lease-up to refinance — is normalized, owner-controlled data flowing across systems in real time. Without it, every operations team is reconciling vendor outputs by hand.

Traditional assets like location and design remain important in today's commercial real estate (CRE) landscape. However, the true differentiator is now data infrastructure. Real-time data lakes, integrated systems, and building intelligence are revolutionizing how properties are operated, maintained, and monetized.​

According to McKinsey & Company, CRE companies that implement data analytics can improve their operating income by up to 15% while simultaneously reducing expenses by 25% .​ (Number Analytics)

The Foundation: Centralized Data Lakes

Centralized data lakes serve as unified repositories that collect real-time information from various building systems, breaking down silos between HVAC, lighting, security, and tenant engagement platforms. This integration provides a single source of truth for operational decisions, enabling predictive analytics that optimize resource use, identify issues before they escalate, and support smarter capital planning.​

The global data lake market is projected to reach USD 90.0 billion by 2032, highlighting the increasing demand for advanced data management and analytics capabilities across industries.​ (Market.us Scoop)


IoT Sensors: Feeding the Intelligence Engine

IoT sensors embedded throughout buildings monitor temperature fluctuations, occupancy patterns, energy consumption, air quality, equipment health, and more. These sensors feed live data into centralized lakes, granting unprecedented visibility into building performance.​

IoT-based predictive maintenance can reduce maintenance costs by up to 25%, with energy savings in commercial buildings reaching up to 30%. (​HashStudioz)

Digital Twins: Simulating Success

Digital twins—virtual replicas of physical spaces—allow operators to simulate scenarios, predict outcomes, and optimize building performance without risk. By creating real-time models of equipment and operations, digital twins can cut unplanned maintenance costs by up to 25%, translating into significant operational savings. (​Planning, Building & Construction Today)

ESG Data Compliance: A Growing Imperative

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting is increasingly mandated by regulators, investors, and tenants. Data infrastructure enables seamless ESG data compliance by automating reporting on energy use, emissions, indoor air quality, and occupancy trends.​

Digital twins contribute to environmental sustainability through resource efficiency, environmental impact assessment, and predictive maintenance.​ (Bright Spaces)

Predictive Maintenance: Protecting the Bottom Line

Predictive maintenance technologies, powered by IoT sensors and AI analytics, have reduced unexpected equipment failures by up to 70%, resulting in maintenance cost savings of 20-40%. This proactive approach reduces downtime, extends asset lifespans, lowers operating costs, and enhances tenant satisfaction—all of which directly impact Net Operating Income (NOI). (​FCIQ)

Final Thoughts

In the new era of commercial real estate, data infrastructure is the engine that drives operational excellence, cost efficiency, regulatory compliance, and tenant loyalty. From IoT sensors to digital twins, the ability to harness real-time intelligence is redefining how buildings are run and how value is created.​

Owners who invest in building intelligence today are setting themselves up for a smarter, more resilient, and more profitable tomorrow.​
