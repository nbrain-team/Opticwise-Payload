Why Intelligent Buildings Are the Next Big Thing for investors

Discover why intelligent buildings are the future of real estate investment, offering efficiency, sustainability, and higher returns for savvy investors.

February 19, 2025 · 3 min read

TL;DR: Investors are pricing intelligence into CRE. Intelligent buildings — built on owner-controlled data and infrastructure — are the next investable category, with measurable operating advantages that show up in cap rates. Buildings without that foundation get priced as legacy stock.

In today’s world buildings can no longer be concrete, steel and glass at its foundation—- not when an intelligent evolution in Commerical real estate is here, not only in design but at its very core. These high-tech structures use AI, automation and IoT to optmize energy consumption, enhance tenant experience and future-proof assets.

As technology advances and sustainability becomes a priority, intelligent buildings are emerging as a key investment for those looking to stay ahead of market demands. Here’s why investors should take notice.

1. Sustainability and Operational Efficiency

Sustainability being a the forefront of this movement it isn’t just a buzzword—it’s becoming a critical consideration for investors. Intelligent buildings integrate smart systems that monitor and control energy consumption, water usage, and waste management.

In fact, according to the World Green Building Council, energy-efficient buildings can reduce operating costs by up to 30%.

With these advanced features, intelligent buildings not only reduce the building’s carbon footprint but also lower operational costs, improving the bottom line. Green building certifications like LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) are increasingly demanded by tenants and investors, making intelligent buildings an attractive asset for long-term investment.

The global green building materials market is expected to reach $577.5 billion by 2027, highlighting the rising demand for sustainable and energy-efficient properties.

2. Enhanced Tenant Experience

The modern tenant has high expectations when it comes to comfort, security, and convenience. Intelligent buildings offer a superior tenant experience by integrating smart technology for everything from lighting and climate control to security and access management.

A study by JLL found that 75% of tenants are more likely to choose office spaces that offer smart building features like personalized climate control, app-based building services, and enhanced security. With features like personalized temperature settings, real-time parking availability, and seamless building access, tenants are more likely to stay in these buildings long-term. Happier tenants translate into higher retention rates and, ultimately, better ROI for investors.

3. Future-Proofing and Market Demand

As technology continues to evolve, intelligent buildings are designed to adapt and scale. They offer built-in flexibility, allowing owners to easily upgrade systems as new technologies emerge. The demand for tech-enabled, sustainable properties is only going to increase.

According to a report by Deloitte, 90% of millennials say sustainability is a key factor in their decision to choose where to live or work, which means intelligent buildings are appealing to younger generations entering the workforce. Additionally, the global smart building market is expected to reach $87.5 billion by 2027, driven by growing demand for more advanced, energy-efficient, and adaptive properties.

4. Data-Driven Insights for Better Decision Making

One of the standout features of intelligent buildings is their ability to collect and analyze data in real-time. These buildings generate vast amounts of data that can be used to optimize building performance, predict maintenance needs, and assess tenant satisfaction.

According to McKinsey, the use of data analytics in smart buildings can reduce operating costs by 10-20% through improved energy management and predictive maintenance. For investors, this means more informed decision-making, from managing operating costs to improving tenant relationships. Data analytics help identify opportunities for cost savings, increase asset value, and enhance overall investment returns.

5. Increased Asset Value and Appeal to Investors

Intelligent buildings are inherently more valuable than traditional buildings due to their operational efficiency, sustainability, and ability to attract and retain tenants. According to CBRE, properties with smart building features can command rents that are 15-20% higher than conventional properties.

They are seen as "future-proof" assets that will hold their value better over time, providing investors with higher long-term returns. Moreover, with the growing emphasis on ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) criteria in investment portfolios, intelligent buildings align perfectly with these values, making them even more appealing to socially responsible investors.

Conclusion

Intelligent buildings are not just a trend; they represent the future of commercial real estate. By embracing the latest technologies in AI, IoT, and sustainability, these properties offer significant advantages for investors. Whether it’s through operational efficiency, improved tenant experiences, or increased asset value, intelligent buildings are poised to deliver long-term financial benefits. According to Statista, the global market for smart buildings is projected to grow at a CAGR of 29.47% from 2023 to 2030, solidifying their role as a major opportunity for forward-thinking investors. For those looking to stay ahead of the curve, investing in intelligent buildings is a smart, future-proof strategy.
