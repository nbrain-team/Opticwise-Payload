The Business Case for Upgrading Digital Infrastructure in Office-to-Residential Conversions

Discover why upgrading digital infrastructure is essential for successful office-to-residential conversions, boosting value, efficiency, and tenant satisfaction.

April 9, 2025 · 4 min read

TL;DR: Office-to-residential conversions live or die on infrastructure. Upgrading digital infrastructure first — connectivity, segmentation, governance — before walls move costs less and unlocks long-term tenant economics. Conversions that skip this step inherit operational debt that follows the asset for decades.

If these walls could talk, they’d ask for better Wi-Fi. The office-to-residential conversion has become the solution for empty buildings, and its not just a temporary fix—it's redefininig urban living. With companies embracing remote and hybrid work, commercial vacancies have skyrocketed. Cities are responding by transforming outdated office spaces into modern apartments, hoping to breathe life back into downtown districts.

In Boston, the number of units underway from office-to-residential conversions increased by 160 percent from 2023 to 2024, totaling 1,167 units (CommercialSearch). This surge highlights the growing demand for repurposed office spaces.

But here’s the catch: Successful conversions involve more than a new floor plan and fresh paint—residential tenants expect a level of digital connectivity that traditional office buildings were never designed for. What worked for a 9-to-5 workspace won’t suffice for a 24/7 home environment. Slow internet, spotty cell service, and outdated wiring can turn a promising conversion into a high vacancy liability.

So, what is the solution? Investing in next-generation digital infrastructure—enterprise-grade connectivity, smart security, and IoT-ready networks–ensures these conversions don’t just fill space but thrive in a competitive rental market.

The Digital Demands of Modern Residents

Today’s renters are looking for a connected lifestyle and not just a place to live—one that is seamless in nature. A staggering 83% of tenants consider reliable internet connectivity the most critical factor in their workplace, a sentiment that undoubtedly extends to their homes (Gensler). Moreover, only 1% of renters currently enjoy uninterrupted Wi-Fi throughout their apartment communities, highlighting a significant gap between expectations and reality (SmartRent). This disconnect can lead to dissatisfaction, higher tenant turnover, and ultimately, a decrease in property value. In the digital age, a home without robust Wi-Fi is like an apartment without running water—unthinkable.

The ROI of Upgrading Digital Infrastructure

Think of digital infrastructure as the foundation of modern living—without it, everything else crumbles. Investing in advanced digital infrastructure isn't just about meeting tenant demands—it's a strategic move that pays dividends:

  • Higher Rents & Occupancy: Properties offering managed Wi-Fi services see a 26-point boost in Net Promoter Scores, indicating higher resident satisfaction and a greater likelihood of attracting and retaining tenants (SmartRent)
  • Lower Vacancy Rates: With office-to-apartment conversions projected to reach an all-time high of 77,700 units in 2025, competition is fierce (Northspyre). Offering superior digital amenities can differentiate a property in a crowded market.
  • Future-Proofing: As remote work persists and smart home technologies become standard, properties equipped with robust digital infrastructure are better positioned to adapt to evolving tenant needs and technological advancements.

Case Studies: Who’s Doing It Right?

Several pioneering projects exemplify the successful integration of advanced digital infrastructure in office-to-residential conversions:

  • New York City's 55 Broad Street: This former office building has been transformed into a residential complex featuring state-of-the-art connectivity, attracting tech-savvy tenants and setting a new standard for urban living (New York Post).
  • San Jose's CityView Plaza: In a bold move, developers are converting four existing office buildings into 320 homes, emphasizing cutting-edge digital amenities to appeal to modern residents (San Francisco Chronicle).

From corporate cubicles to connected condos—these transformations showcase the power of digital reinvention.

Key Upgrades: Where to Invest First

To meet the high expectations of modern tenants, consider prioritizing the following digital infrastructure upgrades:

  1. Enterprise-Grade Wi-Fi & Fiber-Optic Networks: Ensure seamless, high-speed internet access throughout the property, eliminating dead zones and buffering frustrations.
  2. Smart Access & Security Systems: Implement digital key fobs, facial recognition, and AI-powered surveillance to enhance resident safety and convenience.
  3. Energy Efficiency Technologies: Integrate smart thermostats, intelligent lighting, and IoT-driven building controls to promote sustainability and reduce utility costs.
  4. Managed Wi-Fi Services: Offer community-wide Wi-Fi with seamless connectivity, addressing the fact that 79% of renters express interest in such services (SmartRent).

Upgrading your building's tech is like giving it a brain transplant—suddenly, everything just works smarter.

The Competitive Edge: Standing Out in the 2025 Market

With a record 70,700 office-to-residential conversions expected in 2025, standing out is more crucial than ever (CRE Daily). Properties that prioritize digital infrastructure not only attract discerning tenants but also position themselves as forward-thinking leaders in the real estate market. In the race for residents, the fastest Wi-Fi wins.

Conclusion: A Smart Investment for a Smarter Future

Upgrading digital infrastructure in office-to-residential conversions is no longer optional—it's imperative. By investing in robust connectivity and smart technologies, property owners can enhance tenant satisfaction, command higher rents, reduce vacancies, and future-proof their assets. In 2025 and beyond, a building's digital backbone will be just as critical as its physical foundation. Build it smart, and they will come—and stay.

Don’t let outdated digital infrastructure hold your conversion back. Whether you’re transforming an office tower into luxury lofts or a coworking space into cozy condos, connectivity is the new curb appeal. The buildings of tomorrow run on fast, secure, and seamless digital infrastructure—does yours?
